Hi, I'm Graham

I make the hard, ambiguous problems worth solving.

Fourteen years designing products at the intersection of people, systems, and trust. Fintech, marketplaces, real estate, consumer platforms — different domains, same question: what's actually at stake for the person on the other side of this screen?

I think in connections. I find the gap between what a product does and what a person actually needs, then work with teams to close it — through early prototypes, honest conversations, and a lot of iteration. The most useful thing I can do is help a team see clearly and move forward with conviction.

Outside the work, I take cues from everywhere. How a great game earns your trust without a tutorial. How a film establishes stakes in the first ten minutes. How Disney hides every seam so the magic feels inevitable. The designers I follow — Matt D. Smith, Claudio Guglieri, Dann Petty — treat craft as a form of respect for the audience. So do I.

I'm looking for a Staff IC role — large scope, genuinely hard problems, alongside people who care about getting it right.

Approach

01

I think best in the open

I design in a way that invites input early, so progress happens together instead of in isolation.

02

I follow the curiosity

The most interesting problems don't announce themselves. I stay close to what's strange or broken until I understand why.

03

I make ideas visible

Prototypes give teams something real to react to. I'd rather show than describe.

04

I surface decisions and tradeoffs

Naming constraints and choices helps teams commit and move forward with confidence.

05

I look for patterns in unexpected places

Games, films, lived experience — everything is input. The best design moves often come from outside the product.

06

I iterate with others, not alone

The best ideas come through conversation. I prototype early so we have something real to argue about.
